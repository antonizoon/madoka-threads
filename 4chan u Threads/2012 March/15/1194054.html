>continued, tl;dr etc
8th, who is a very solid translator, had a decent scale for that, "LS" meaning "liberal scale". LS-1 would basically be a direct word-for-word dictionary translation, like what a bad machine translation would do. LS-10 would be the opposite end, extreme localization getting close to the point of script writing. Getting more liberal makes it harder to stay accurate (but not impossible, "liberal" != "bad"), but too low and it's almost gibberish vs actual English. The sweet spot between these two, for me at least, is usually LS-5/LS-6. Sometimes a little lower or higher is fine. Some subbers pretty explicitly have localizing as a goal, gg in many cases for example, so they tend to be more around LS-8. When they do a good job that can still be plenty accurate and have impressive flow, but it's easy to lose bits too and it's a bit jarring. Shit guesslation/dictionary users are down around 1/2.

In this context, I consider tri4 to be around LS-3.5, although full disclosure, I've only watched some of theirs, not the whole series, maybe I'll change my mind. That's too low for me to find it as enjoyable. I can do really literal stuff with my own understanding of the language, part of the fun for me is seeing how other translators phrased something tricky, and the different English meaning people pull out. I only speak for myself though, and again, really do appreciate the effort tri4 put in to this whole thing. If you like theirs, awesome.

Since we're talking about the VN aspect of the game, people need to remember how canon for a VN differs from anime. Take F/SN for example. Shirou in the anime is not the kind of person who'd back down from his ideals or run away from an important fight. But for the purposes of the VN, you have options where he does both of these, leading to bad ends. It's just a necessary part of the game where you have choices that are wrong. If you pick those that lead to the good/true ends, then you get the anime canon Shirou who is the kind of guy that will fight for his ideals to the end...and that is the way he'll no doubt stay depicted canonically in anime or any media that has a single route, like manga etc.
